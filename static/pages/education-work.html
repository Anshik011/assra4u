Why Education

If we need to address healthcare, poverty, population control, unemployment and human rights, there's no better way to start than providing education to children in need. Education not only empowers children to have a secure future but also helps them grow up as responsible national and global citizens.

The Right to Education (RTE) Act which came into force in 2010 made education free and compulsory for all children in the age group of 6-14 years. But even a decade later, the learning curve has not been steady for many children in the country.

The socio-economic conditions of parents and lack of proper learning in schools are hindrances which prevent many children from having quality education.

Basic Education Program

What We Do

ASSRA’s flagship basic education initiative works with the objective of empowering underprivileged children in rural and marginalized communities by providing supplementary education, nutritional support, and wellness care.

The program is aligned with the New Education Policy (NEP) 2020, and the SDG Goal 4 (Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all).

Our program supports children (3-18 years) living in tribal belts, remote villages, and hard-to-reach areas, as well as drop-outs and working children of migrant labor in urban clusters, helping them reintegrate into mainstream education.

Our Pillars of Work

4-Pronged Approach

Improving Learning Outcomes

We work to enhance the learning quality and outcomes of children in classrooms through interactive curriculum modifications, activities, and supplemental digital courses.

  • Curriculum Based Learning
  • STEM & Experiential Learning
  • Digital Learning Solutions
  • Sports and Extra-Curricular Activities
  • Nutrition and Healthcare Support

Creating Enabling Learning Environment

A safe, clean, and inspiring physical infrastructure is crucial for child retention in schools. We help construct and refurbish classrooms to foster clean and vibrant schools.

  • Infrastructure Development
  • Using Building as Learning Aid (BALA)
  • Solar Panels for Alternative Power
  • Refurbishment of Classrooms & Labs
  • Setting up STEM & Numeracy Labs

Capacity Building of Teachers

Empowering educators with pedagogical methods and digital solutions enables a lasting system of quality teaching in local schools.

  • Classroom Management
  • Subject Specific Knowledge
  • Child Pedagogy and Wellbeing
  • Teaching Learning Tools
  • Use of Digital Tools

Community Engagement

Aligning parents, local leaders, and school councils with the goal of child literacy helps secure children's long-term educational retention.

  • Engaging Local Stakeholders
  • Parent Teachers Associations
  • School Management Committees
