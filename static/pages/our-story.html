About ASSRA

Our Story

ASSRA (Association for Social Service and Rehabilitation of the Aged) was initiated in 2002 with the primary mission of rehabilitating destitute senior citizens and providing them with care, security, and dignity. Driven by the philosophy of creating a compassionate society, a group of dedicated professionals came together to build a structured mechanism for grassroots social development.

As we worked closely with marginalized families, we realized that social challenges cannot be solved in isolation. A grandfather's health is linked to a grandchild's education, and a mother's financial empowerment secures the entire household. This realization led us to expand our intervention areas beyond elderly care to include basic child education, sustainable rural livelihoods, and environmental protection.

Over the last two decades, ASSRA has evolved into a highly transparent, community-focused social institution. Operating under a lifecycle development model, we run educational centres for tribal children in Jharkhand and migrant laborers' children in Delhi, vocational training programs for women, and agro-forestry campaigns to combat deforestation, bringing lasting change to the ground.

Our Mission

Empowering Communities, Elevating Futures

Our mission acts as a guiding compass to resolve deep-seated developmental imbalances by targeting root causes and deploying innovative, modern solutions.

ASSRA is committed to empower destitute elderly, underprivileged children, youth, and women through dedicated care, relevant education, and market-focused livelihood programmes.

We deploy best possible methodologies and promote strict transparency for achieving high social return on investment (SROI), practicing absolute accountability and good governance.

We link corporate social responsibility with grassroots community needs, sensitizing the privileged civil society to participate actively in sustainable social development.

Community Driven Change

While our focus as a development organisation has been first and foremost to work on the ground for bringing positive change in the lives of the destitute aged and underprivileged families, we make equal efforts to sustain this change by engaging the wider civil society in social responsibility.

Not remaining restricted to direct intervention, ASSRA advocates for collective responsibility, making local communities, administration, and corporate partners active stakeholders in all our welfare and rehabilitation initiatives.

Education Cannot Be Done in Isolation

But while working on the ground, we realised that child education cannot be done in isolation, without ensuring the welfare of the whole family. Health is also a part of education. A child will not go to school if he is sick. If a child’s parents are afflicted with health problems, he might drop-out of school and start earning instead. Unless the mother is healthy and empowered, the child cannot be either. It is all interlinked.

Our other programmes evolved as a logical progression to our education programme – be it livelihood, health or women empowerment. Today, children and education continue to remain at the centre of all we do, but through the lifecycle approach we also address the needs of the children’s families and communities.

How We Work

A Structured Framework to Maximise Ground Impact

Cooperative Model

Cooperative & SHG Networks

ASSRA partners with community-based groups and Self-Help Groups (SHGs) to identify and implement development projects on the ground. We handhold these groups, building their capacities in financial management, vocational trades, and organic farming, ensuring that solutions become self-sustaining and scalable over time.

Direct Implementation

Direct Grassroots Action

Under our direct action model, ASSRA directly establishes and manages shelters for the aged, educational centres in tribal villages, and agro-forestry nurseries. This ensures strict quality control, professional project execution, and transparent utilization of resources for maximum social return on investment.

Transparency

Credibility and Governance Benchmarks

Credibility and financial transparency are core pillars of ASSRA. We adhere to the highest standards of governance through regular internal audits, published annual reports, and standard operational reviews, ensuring that every donation is fully accounted for and directly supports beneficiaries.

Governance Matrix

Our dual-audit and community assessment systems track the efficiency of our shelters, crop yields, and child learning progress, guaranteeing absolute transparency and structural accountability for all our stakeholders.
