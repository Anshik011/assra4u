Why Environment

Our future depends on how we protect our planet today. Issues like rapid deforestation, plastic pollution, climate change, and dwindling clean water resources directly threaten vulnerable rural and tribal populations. Ensuring a clean, green, and sustainable ecosystem is vital for human survival.

At ASSRA, we believe environmental health is linked to community development. Our campaigns support cleanliness, plantation, solar energy adoption, and eco-awareness in schools to build a climate-resilient future.

We work to plant native trees, reduce solid waste, and install off-grid solar lamps to directly improve quality of life while reducing carbon footprints.

What We Do

ASSRA conducts agro-forestry campaigns in the Patamda block of East Singhbhum (Jharkhand) to combat deforestation, promoting tree plantations of Tick, Gambhar, Sisam, Mango, and Eucalyptus.

We train local communities in hilly crop cultivation methods to prevent soil erosion and improve agricultural yields sustainably.

We organize mass awareness meetings to engage rural youth in environmental campaigns, creating community-led movements to check deforestation and conserve local biodiversity.

Our Pillars of Work

4-Pronged Approach

Cleanliness & Waste

Organizing cleanliness drives, setup of community dustbins, sorting dry and wet waste, and implementing local composting for agricultural use.

  • Waste Segregation Systems
  • Cleanliness & Sanitization Drives
  • Bio-Compost Preparation
  • Plastic Reduction Programs
  • Community Dustbins Setup

Tree Plantation

Planting native, soil-friendly trees in local community fields, forest zones, and city green patches to fight air pollution and global warming.

  • Native Species Selection
  • Regular Sapling Maintenance
  • Fruit Orchard Development
  • Urban Micro-Forests
  • Corporate Green Partnerships

Renewable Energy

Deploying solar streetlights and distributing fuel-efficient smokeless stoves in off-grid tribal regions to safeguard environment and health.

  • Solar Streetlights Install
  • Home Solar Lanterns
  • Clean Cook-Stoves Distribution
  • Village Energy Committees
  • Maintenance Skills Workshops

Eco-Clubs in Schools

Engaging children in schools with climate education, rainwater harvesting demos, organic kitchen gardening, and eco-competitions.

  • School Green Competitions
  • Rainwater Harvesting Demos
  • Kitchen Garden Projects
  • Eco-Sensitization Camps
  • Waste-to-Art Workshops
